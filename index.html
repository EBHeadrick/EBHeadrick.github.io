Life Insurance

Life Insurance is the one kind of insurance that will always be used. There are many variables to consider when this kind of product, for it can be used as a protection and source of stability in today's unpredictable world. For children, life insurance can be a way that parents or grandparents set up a fund that can be used tax-free later in life for education, or for major purchases. During young adulthood it can be used to provide a spouse with income in case of premature death. In middle age, it can be used to protect a spouse, children, or to pay off a mortgage. In old age, life insurance can be used to provide for final expenses. What many people don't realize is that life insurance can be used to fund a retirement with tax free income. Life insurance has many benefits for the living too! One thing important to understand is that the younger you are when you start a life insurance policy, the less it will cost.

At Boulder Insurance Group, we specialize in helping our clients to secure policies that cover them throughout the major events in their lives, giving them the answers and peace of mind that they seek when planning for the future.

Term

Term Life Insurance costs less than whole life insurance, but does not build cash value. It lasts for a specific number of years, or term. Usually the term is 10, 20, or 30 years. The cost is based on age at issue, and stays the same thru out the term. Some term policies can be converted to whole life without evidence of insurability. This is an excellent way to start out with the coverage amount you need, and convert to whole life as your financial situation improves.

At Boulder Insurance Group, our expert consultants know how to help you best structure your life insurance policies to maximize your security and benefit.

Whole

Whole Life Insurance is for your whole life. The cost is determined based on your age when the policy is issued, and it lasts until the end of your life. Some policies are paid up in one lump sum, some have premiums for 10 or 20 years, and some are paid up at age 65. Whole life policies build cash value that can be used later. Money can be surrendered, or taken out as a loan, but be careful! If money is not drawn out properly, it can be a taxable event, or even cause the policy to lapse prematurely.

Our agents can provide you with the resources to compare different life insurance options and learn what you need to know to make sure that the coverage you choose works in your favor.

Long Term Care Insurance

Long Term Care is Chronic Care. Chronic Care can be defined as Care given over a long time period without the expectation of the patient returning to good health.

Long Term Care can include services that are medical and non-medical in nature, and provide for the health and well being of the patient. Often, Long Term Care does not require skilled medical professionals; it begins at home with assistance provided by family members, neighbors, or friends. Loved ones acting as caregivers deliver Custodial Care, which includes transportation, shopping, managing finances, and help with the 6 activities of daily living. These activities of daily living are clearly defined in the medical, and insurance community; They are eating, bathing dressing, toileting, continence, and transferring (eg. Moving from bed to a chair). Sometimes a patient will require constant observation and assistance for dementia.

Long Term Care can be provided in a variety of settings:

  • Long Term CareHome Care
  • Nursing Home
  • Assisted Living Facilities
  • Skilled Nursing Facilities
  • Hospice Facilities
are the most common places to receive care. Because Long Term Care usually does not require the assistance of skilled medical professionals, it is usually not covered by health insurance or by Medicare.

Most Long Term Care is financed by private funds (self-insurance), Medicaid, or by Long Term Care Insurance. The insurance specialists at Boulder Insurance Group have years of experience working in most settings and scenarios involving Long Term Care, and Long Term Care Insurance. We will strive to find a suitable solution to your unique situation.

Many of our new clients are unaware that policies can be structured in such a way that any unused premiums can be returned to a beneficiary. They can also be structured to be paid up within a certain time period, 10 or 20 years for example.

Long Term Care Insurance is not the only solution to funding Long Term Care needs, in fact there are options available that can provide for long term growth of principle, increased death benefit, and Long Term Care.

Disability Insurance

Disability Insurance is the foundation of any financial plan. It protects the source of funding for all of the other planning. Despite the importance of Disability Insurance, it is the most commonly overlooked component of a protection strategy. A person in their 40's is almost twice as likely to lose income for 90 days or more due to a disability, than they are to lose income due to premature death.

Boulder Insurance Group is dedicated to helping our customers choose the correct mix of coverages and plans for their present and future needs. No one plans to utilize a disability insurance policy, but having strong coverages in place will protect you against the unexpected.

Long Term Disability

Long Term Disability Insurance is intended to replace income during an extended illness, or rehabilitation. None of us expect to become seriously injured. In fact, like Life Insurance, people often overlook securing disability coverage because they just don't want to deal with the unpleasant thought of needing to use it. However, having protection against the unexpected can prevent hassles, skyrocketing medical bills, and heartache during a time of crisis. At Boulder Insurance Group, we believe that worrying about medical expenses and lost in come should be the last thing on your mind when you're recovering from a long-term illness or injury. We can work with you to set up a plan that will bring peace of mind you need to restore your health as soon as possible.

Long term disability insurance has a longer elimination period than short term disability insurance, usually 90 or 180 days. The policies can last for 2 years or more, even until age 65.

Contact us at Boulder Insurance Group and we can discuss whether adding long term disability insurance to your financial blueprint is the right move for you.

Short Term Disability

In the event of an unexpected accident, you might find yourself unable to work for a short period of time. If you do not have enough paid leave from work, you might experience an interruption in your income. Short Term Disability Insurance is designed to fill in the gaps while you recover.

After a short elimination period, usually 3 days, the policy will begin to pay a daily benefit directly to the individual. This money can then be used to pay bills that would normally be paid from the lost income. There are limits to what percentage of income can be replaced, and there are strategies to maximize this income. Short Term Disability Insurance usually only lasts for 90 days; if coverage is needed for a longer period, Long Term Disability Insurance kicks in. There are many short term disability providers and plans to choose from.

Estate Planning

Estate planning includes wills, directives, and trusts. The basic concept is to maximize the money that goes to your family or favorite charity, and to ensure that your final wishes are carried out. You should seek the help of a legal professional before taking any action on the legal issues involved in estate planning. Boulder Insurance Group does not give legal advice, but encourages all of our customers to engage in at least minimal estate planning activities. To this end, we have provided some more detail below. The following is for informational purposes, to support our valued customers: Estate Planning - Wills A will is a legal document that determines the succession of your estate. Without a will, the state will distribute the entire estate according to predetermined rules. In other words, your final wishes may not be carried out the way you desire unless your estate planning documents contain a will. For families with small children, your will is to provide for your children in the event of your premature death. In addition to providing for their care and upbringing, the guardian will have control of the proceeds from any life insurance policies until they reach maturity. This is a very broad comment on the very complicated subject of estate planning so please seek legal counsel. In general life insurance companies will not distribute life insurance proceeds to a minor. Estate Planning - Directives Directives are a critical part of estate planning if you want to have your wishes carried out, and not put tremendous decision making burdens on your loved ones. Directives cover issues like Do Not Resuscitate Orders, medical and financial Power of Attorney, and final wishes. This part of Estate Planning allows a person to name someone to act on their behalf in financial or medical situations when they are not able to do so themselves. Estate Planning is the process of helping people ensure that their assets are distributed according to their wishes. In the case of a complex estate, complex planning can be used to minimize the amount of estate tax due upon the person’s death. Boulder Insurance Group’s estate planning experts can help you navigate this complex issue and choose the most relevant structure for you. We’re also able to work directly with your financial advisor, attorney and/or accountant to meet your estate planning needs.

Living Trust

Trusts are an arrangement where real or personal property is held by a trustee for the benefit of another person (the beneficiary). There are two basic kinds of trusts; testamentary trusts are formed at your death and a living trust is established during your lifetime. In a funded living trust, the settlor puts money and property into their trust during their lifetime for their own benefit, and possibly for the benefit of others. When a person is capable of managing their own affairs, they usually name themselves as trustee of the living trust they establish. The duties of the trustee are to invest and distribute the assets of the trust to the settlor and beneficiaries. Choosing a Revocable or Irrevocable Living Trust. Living trusts are almost always revocable, which means the settlor can revoke or amend the trust as long as they live, or as long as they are capable of managing their own affairs. Alternate trustees can be named to take over the trustee duties if the settlor becomes disabled or unable to perform their duties. When the settlor dies, the living trust usually terminates, and the successor trustee distributes the trust property to the beneficiaries, similar to the distribution from a will. Often times, a living trust will continue after the death of the settlor in order to provide benefits to the settlor’s spouse and family. The living trust will then terminate at a later date. If a living trust is set up properly, and fully funded (all of the settlor’s assets are put into the trust), there can be several advantages. A fully funded living trust can eliminate the need for probate upon the death of the settlor. If a settlor owns property in multiple states, it can possibly eliminate the need for probate in multiple states. A living trust may eliminate the need for conservatorship if the settlor becomes disabled or unable to fulfill the duties of trustee. However, a separate medical power of attorney is needed in order to make medical decisions for the settlor. The terms of a living trust are generally private whereas the terms of a will are public. Your Living Trust and Taxes: If a living trust is not drafted correctly, there can be significant harmful tax liabilities. It’s usually better to have professional assistance in drafting either a testamentary or living trust instead of trying to do it yourself, or by purchasing a preprinted trust form. It’s best for you to keep an up to date list of all your property and debts. Things to include are insurance policies, securities, bank accounts, real estate, artwork, jewelry, business interests, pension plans, IRA’s, and other retirement benefits. You should keep this list with your other important documents. It’s best to keep a list of your current financial advisors, your attorney, and your accountant in a safe, easy to find place also. There are many variables to weigh in the creation of a living trust so professional help is a must to be sure your wishes are honored.
